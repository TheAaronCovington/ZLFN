# Writing Logical Expressions

You can enter expressions using formal symbols or ASCII equivalents.

Operators (by precedence):
- Negation: `¬` or `!` or `~`
- Conjunction: `∧` or `&`
- Disjunction: `∨` or `|`
- Implication: `→` or `->` or `=>`
- Biconditional: `↔` or `<->` or `<=>`

Examples:
- `(A ∧ B) → C`
- `!(P | Q) & R`
- `X <-> (Y -> Z)`

Tips:
- Use parentheses to control grouping.
- Variables may contain letters, digits, and `_` (e.g., `A1`, `foo_bar`).
